How Laws Are Organized

Laws in the United States exist at both the federal and state level. Federal laws apply nationwide, while state laws apply only within each state.

Federal Laws

Federal laws are passed by Congress and enforced across all states. They cover areas such as civil rights, taxes, immigration, and national security.

State Laws

Each state has its own set of laws and penalties. These often cover criminal law, traffic law, education, and local governance.
