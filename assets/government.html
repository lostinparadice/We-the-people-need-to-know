Why Government Exists

Governments exist to protect rights, create and enforce laws, provide public services, and resolve disputes.

The Constitution

The U.S. Constitution is the highest law in the country. It defines government powers, limits authority, and explains how leaders are chosen.

The Three Branches

Legislative Branch (Congress)

Writes laws, controls spending, and oversees the executive branch.

Executive Branch (President)

Enforces laws, manages federal agencies, and conducts foreign policy.

Judicial Branch (Courts)

Interprets laws and decides whether they follow the Constitution.

Checks and Balances

Power is divided so no branch becomes too strong. Each branch can limit the others.

Federal vs State Power

The federal government handles national issues. States manage local matters like education, policing, and transportation.

Why this matters: Understanding government helps people vote responsibly, evaluate policies, and hold leaders accountable.
