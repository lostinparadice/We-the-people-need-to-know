Understanding Inflation

What it is, why it happens, and why it matters

What Is Inflation?

Inflation is the rise in prices over time. When inflation happens, the same amount of money buys fewer goods and services.

Why Inflation Happens

  • More money in circulation
  • Higher production or transportation costs
  • Supply shortages
  • Increased demand

How Inflation Is Measured

Governments track inflation using price indexes, which measure changes in the cost of common goods and services over time.

Why Inflation Matters

Inflation affects wages, savings, rent, food prices, and interest rates. High inflation can reduce purchasing power and financial stability.

Example: If groceries cost $100 last year and $108 this year, that represents roughly 8% inflation for those items.
